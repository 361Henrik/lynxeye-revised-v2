Phase 3 bridge

What becomes possible after the value pilot proves what works.

Phase 3 stays interesting without becoming premature. The bridge makes it visible as a value pathway: scale proven behavior, automate repeatable workflows, and build new capability only where evidence is strong enough.

Phase 1

Scope & Setup

Clarify current practice, HQ infrastructure, safety boundaries, pain points, priority people, and the value hypotheses worth testing.

Phase 2

Value Pilot

Work inside real Lynxeye workflows, coach people and teams, capture examples, and prove where AI improves quality, speed, confidence or capacity.

Phase 3

Scale, Automate, Build

Scale what works, automate what repeats, connect what fits HQ infrastructure, and explore new AI-enabled client offerings.

Automation should not carry the pilot. Automation belongs after the pilot has shown which workflows are valuable, repeatable, and safe enough to scale.

Why orchestration

Two strategies for AI: ping-ponging, or building capability.

Most people start with ping-pong mode: ask, answer, retry, move on. It can be useful, but the value stays personal, inconsistent, and hard to scale.

Ping-pong with AI chat One person tries something, gets an answer, edits it, and moves on. The next person starts again from scratch.
Scale and orchestrate with AI The workflow is designed with context, approved tools, examples, review points, quality checks, and playbooks.
Company capability Learning becomes shared practice, not hidden individual experimentation.
HQ and Eraneos create the secure foundation. Lynxeye creates value when that foundation becomes smooth daily use, stronger client projects, faster pitches, and company-owned methods.
